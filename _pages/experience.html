Robotics Intern (Personal Project) | Reubotics, Millbrae, CA

November 2023 - Present

1. Designed (using Onshape's CAD software), assembled, machined, and programmed a Robot with the guidance of the Robotics Subject Matter Expert, Dr. Reuben Brewer. The purpose of the robot is to detect, pick up, and sort trash lying on the road.
2. Used Dynamixel AX-18A and XC-330 Motors for this project. Setup communication to motors through the Pyserial module, given the packet length, instruction packets, parity, etc.
3. Parts used in the robot included 8mm shafts, flanged ball bearings, clamping shaft collars, shims, and an assortment of socket head screws. Tools used included Allen wrenches, hand taps, reamers, and a hand drill. All main parts were 3-D printed on a Bambu Lab X1 Carbon printer.
4. Stress-tested the design in the CAD software (Onshape) and calculated expected torque on the motor. This was used to make the gear ratios torque-oriented.
5. Cut, re-lengthened, and soldered wires in order to make the wires fit my design.
6. Wrote a program using Computer Vision concepts to train a custom Machine Learning Model (YOLOv5 Object Detection Model) to help the Robot "track" and detect the plastic bottle and move towards it.

Skills: Design, CAD, Kinematics, Trigonometry, Python, Computer Vision

Radio Frequency (Wireless Networks) Engineering Intern | Verizon
(Through the NAF Academy of Engineering internship program)

Jun 2024 – July 2024

1. Analyzed the performance of Verizon's 5G Fixed Wireless Access (FWA) Products (on C-band) in several counties in Northern California. Verizon's Fixed Wireless Access product, also known as 5G Home Internet, provides 5G internet access without needing a cabled connection to one's house.
2.Troubleshooted and resolved issues such as slow internet speeds, high drop rates, and radio frequency interference at 5G sites, which resulted in better Internet experiences for customers. Issues can include an incorrectly built cell site, an overshooting antenna tilt, or a synchronization issue.
3. Worked on the steps that go into planning, building, and maintaining a cell site. These include cell site design (simulating antenna coverage based on power, height, and azimuth), power regulation, safety inspection (Electromagnetic Exposure), municipal engagement, and system performance. Also learnt about the difference between 5G SA (Standalone) and 5G NSA (Non Stand Alone), TDD (Time Division Duplexing) vs. FDD (Frequency Division Duplexing), Cell Site Guard Periods, as well as Quadrature Amplitude Modulation (QAM).
4. This internship was for 6 weeks (32 hours per week).
